Experiences in Undergrad

Opportunities unlike any other

Undergraduate is a time to experience a variety of different environments before commiting to one.

Research Experience in Undergraduate (REU)

Research Experiences in undergraduate are a good way to do research as an undergraduate. Whether you're planning on going to graduate school, or work in a research and development position, an REU can be a valuable experience for any student. Not only do they provide great experiences for research, an REU is a great place to network with other undergraduates with similar interests. In addition, most REUs provide summer housing and a decent stipend.

In addition to summer research experiences, most professors are doing research on campus year round. If you're interested in participating in research, talk to one of your professors! Many professors are happy to develop a research project for a student. Many of the mathematics majors here at Western are participating in research.

Internships

Internships are fantastic way to get a feel for a job. Whether it be a software engineering internship or web development, many companies offer internships to current undergraduates. An internship typically consists of one or more summer projects that the company needs done. These can be anywhere from tasks that full-time members don't have time for, to jobs that would best be suited for an intern. Internships are a fantastic way to network within your field of interest, and a great way to discover what the real world is like. While internships pay fairly well, not all companies provide housing for interns. Make sure to find out what all is provided by the company and what you'll need to provide.
